About the job
Senior Water Resources Project Manager

A well-established, employee-focused infrastructure consulting firm is seeking a Senior Water Resources Project Manager to support and lead Federal water resources projects in the Orlando area.

This is a leadership role within a growing Water Resources team, focused primarily on hydrologic and hydraulic modeling, floodplain mapping, watershed evaluations, and FEMA-related studies.

Key Responsibilities:
Lead and oversee Federal water resources projects of varying size and complexity
Manage and mentor a team of engineers and technical staff
Prepare and review hydrologic and hydraulic studies in support of floodplain management
Conduct detailed model reviews to ensure compliance with local, state, and federal standards
Review floodplain and floodway mapping for FEMA LOMC applications
Perform technical reviews of GIS data supporting H&H analyses
Prepare reports, calculations, and technical documentation
Coordinate closely with clients and regulatory agencies
Develop proposals, scopes of work, and fee estimates
Manage project scope, schedule, and budget
Support stormwater and drainage design projects, including system retrofits and new infrastructure

Required Qualifications:
10+ years of experience delivering water resources projects, including mid-to-large scale Federal projects
Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Civil or Environmental Engineering (ABET accredited)
Active Florida Professional Engineer (PE) license
Strong experience in surface water modeling and floodplain mapping
Proficiency with ArcGIS and industry-standard H&H software
Experience working directly with government agencies at local and state levels
Hands-on stormwater infrastructure design experience
Strong understanding of project planning and execution strategy
Proven proposal development and client relationship experience
Excellent written and verbal communication skills
Demonstrated project management experience with municipal stormwater projects

Preferred Qualifications:
Certified Floodplain Manager (CFM) certification (or ability to obtain within one year)
Experience with FEMA modeling, mapping criteria, and map revisions (LOMR, CLOMR, PMR)
Proficiency with additional H&H software tools
Experience leading and developing technical teams
Business development experience within the water resources sector

About the Firm:
The firm is a multidisciplinary infrastructure consulting organization with a strong presence across multiple states and a long-standing reputation in water resources and stormwater services. The water resources team includes a large group of engineers, floodplain specialists, and GIS professionals delivering integrated solutions for municipal, state, and federal clients.
The company offers a collaborative, team-oriented culture with a strong emphasis on professional development and long-term growth.
Compensation is competitive and commensurate with experience. Comprehensive benefits package included.
