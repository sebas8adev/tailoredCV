About the job
SUMMARY

The Technical Project Manager (TPM) plays a pivotal role in driving strategic technology initiatives across our community bank and its fintech payments subsidiary. This role acts as a critical liaison between traditional banking operations and cutting-edge digital payment solutions, ensuring seamless project execution that aligns with regulatory compliance, cybersecurity standards, and customer-first innovation.


ESSENTIAL DUTIES

Lead end-to-end project lifecycle management for complex technical initiatives, from ideation through deployment and post-launch optimization.
Collaborate with stakeholders across banking and fintech domains to define project scope, KPIs, timelines, and success metrics.
Coordinate cross-functional teams including IT, InfoSec, compliance, operations, QA, and third-party vendors to ensure alignment and accountability.
Monitors individual team member progress, proactively identifies blockers, and enforces accountability for meeting project deadlines through structured follow-ups, performance tracking tools, and milestone-based reporting.
Ensure all projects meet or exceed regulatory, cybersecurity, and data privacy standards.
Manages vendor relationships and third-party risk, including SLAs, and compliance audits,
Facilitate and participate in Agile ceremonies (daily standups, sprint planning, retrospectives) and champion Scrum best practices.
Oversee integration of core banking systems with digital platforms including APIs, mobile apps, cloud-native services, and third-party SaaS solutions.
Drive continuous improvement through retrospectives, process automation, and adoption of project management tools (e.g., Jira, Confluence).
Develop and maintain detailed project plans, Gantt charts, resource forecasts, and RAID logs.
Proactively identify and mitigate risks, resolve blockers, and ensure timely delivery of milestones.
Monitor and report on project performance using dashboards, OKRs, and real-time analytics.
Communicate project status, risks, and dependencies to executive leadership and key stakeholders with clarity and transparency.
Ensure technical feasibility and architectural alignment with enterprise IT strategy and digital transformation goals.
Foster a culture of innovation, accountability, and operational excellence.
Support internal and external audits, preparing documentation and ensuring controls are audit ready.
Provide backup support for departmental functions during staff absences or peak workloads.
Demonstrate ethical leadership, integrity, and commitment to organizational values.
Promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in team collaboration and hiring practices.
Ensure full compliance with all internal policies and applicable federal/state banking regulations, including BSA/AML.
Maintain a safe and compliant work environment in accordance with OSHA and workplace safety standards.

SECONDARY DUTIES

The position of Technical Project Manager performs duties specific to the position and other functions as assigned.

SUPERVISORY RESPONSIBILITY

While this position does not have direct supervisory authority, the Technical Project Manager is responsible for driving accountability across cross-functional teams by tracking individual progress, identifying blockers, and ensuring timely delivery of project milestones. TPM influences without authority by fostering ownership, providing structured follow-ups, and escalating risks when necessary.
