Job Description:
About the job
Progressive is dedicated to helping employees move forward and live fully in their careers. Your journey has already begun. Apply today and take the first step to Destination: Progress.

As an IT project manager within the Enterprise Resource Project Manager office (ERPMO) of the Mergers & Acquisitions (M&A) Aligned Delivery Organization, you’ll lead complex, multi-platform enterprise projects that support Progressive’s strategic growth through M&A initiatives. You’ll manage projects of varying size and complexity, ensuring alignment with scope, cost, schedule, and quality throughout the lifecycle. You’ll collaborate with executive sponsors, stakeholders, and cross-functional teams and lead planning, execution, and continuous improvement using Agile and Lean practices. This role offers long-term growth potential and delivers value on strategic initiatives. A key responsibility includes partnering with the Corporate Development group and functional leaders across the business to enable acquisition-led growth and integrations that expand Progressive’s core operations.

This a remote position with occasional travel to a meeting and/or training.

Must-have Qualifications

Minimum 4 years of work experience managing professionals, technologies, architecture, or project delivery.
Bachelor's degree or higher in an Information Technology or Business discipline or related field of study (e.g., Computer Science, Business Management) and a minimum of 1 year of work experience leading or managing professionals, technologies, architecture, or project delivery.

Preferred Skills

Comprehensive Methodology Knowledge with demonstrated experience with various delivery methodologies such as Waterfall, Hybrid, Agile, Scrum, Kanban, and LEAN approaches, enabling tailored delivery strategies across diverse project environments.
M&A Project Leadership in leading teams and organizations through the complexities of mergers and acquisitions, balancing innovation with adaptability to meet evolving demands and tight timelines.
Strong communication skills with the ability to manage expectations and build trust across sponsors, senior leaders, and cross-functional teams.
Proven ability to align diverse teams, foster inclusivity, promote a culture of learning, and drive seamless integration of processes, technologies, and cultures during organizational transitions.
Adept at resolving competing priorities, negotiating win-win outcomes, and maintaining focus on strategic objectives under pressure.
